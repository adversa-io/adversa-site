Getting Started with Adversa

Adversa helps you monitor competitor websites and receive AI-powered summaries whenever something changes. Here is how to get set up in a few minutes.

1. Create a monitor

Start by creating a new monitor inside Adversa. Each monitor tracks a single competitor page and checks it periodically for changes. You can create as many monitors as your plan allows, covering different competitors or different pages from the same competitor.

2. Add the competitor page URL

Enter the URL of the page you want to track. Good starting points include competitor pricing pages, product pages, feature lists, changelogs, and blog posts. Any page that changes meaningfully over time is worth monitoring.

If you are not sure which pages to add first, see the guide on what competitor pages are most useful to monitor.

3. Choose monitoring frequency

Adversa checks the monitored page periodically and compares each new snapshot against the previous version. You can choose how frequently Adversa checks — daily monitoring is a good default for most competitor pages, giving you timely updates without unnecessary noise.

4. Receive alerts and AI summaries

When Adversa detects a change, it highlights exactly what is different and generates a concise AI summary explaining what changed and why it might matter for your business. Instead of wading through a raw diff, you get a plain-language explanation of the update delivered straight to your inbox.

To understand how these summaries are generated, see how AI change summaries work.
